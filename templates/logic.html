Algorithm Logic

This page documents exactly how every signal is generated — the data sources, the indicators, the scoring weights, and the risk management rules. Nothing is a black box.

Signal Generation Pipeline

1

Volume Filter (Gate)

Fetch 5-minute candles for ~100 liquid S&P 500 stocks via FMP real-time API (with yfinance fallback). Calculate time-of-day-adjusted Relative Volume (RVOL). Stocks with RVOL < 1.33x are immediately discarded — insufficient institutional interest.

2

Technical Scoring (65% weight)

Score each surviving stock on 5 technical dimensions: EMA alignment, RSI momentum zone, MACD trend, VWAP position, and Bollinger Band position. Each dimension gets 15-25 points, normalized to 0-100.

3

News Sentiment Scoring (25% weight)

Pull recent news headlines from Finnhub. Score each headline with VADER (finance-tuned NLP). Apply recency decay: news <15 min old gets full weight, >1 hour old gets 30% weight. Aggregate into -1.0 to +1.0 sentiment score.

4

Composite Score & Ranking

Composite = (Technical × 0.65) + (Sentiment × 0.25) + (Volume × 0.10). Stocks scoring below 60 are dropped. Top 10 are displayed, ranked by score.

5

Trade Level Calculation

For each signal: Entry = current price. Stop-loss = Entry − (ATR × 2.0). ATR Target = Entry + (stop distance × 2.5). Resistance Target = nearest pivot-point resistance (R1/R2/R3) above entry, calculated from prior day's High/Low/Close using the Standard Floor Pivot formula. Position size = 1% account risk / stop distance. Hard close by 3:55 PM ET.

6

Signal Persistence & History

Every signal is saved to a cumulative daily log (Today's Finds). Signals accumulate across all scans throughout the day, giving a complete picture of what triggered. A 3-day rolling history is maintained so you can review patterns and compare across sessions. Files older than 3 days are automatically cleaned up.

Technical Indicators

EMA Alignment

Bullish when 9-EMA > 21-EMA > 50-EMA. This confirms a multi-timeframe uptrend. Full points (25) for triple alignment, partial for 9 > 21 only.

Periods: 9, 21, 50
RSI Momentum

Sweet spot is 55–75: strong momentum without being overbought. Below 50 is bearish. Above 75 is overbought — reduced conviction.

Period: 14 | Zone: 55–75
MACD

Histogram positive AND growing = bullish acceleration. Just positive = bullish but decelerating. Negative but improving = early reversal signal.

Fast: 12 | Slow: 26 | Signal: 9
VWAP

Volume-Weighted Average Price — the institutional benchmark. Price above VWAP = net buyers dominating. >0.5% above = strong conviction.

Intraday calculation
Bollinger Bands

Position within bands indicates momentum. Upper half with room to run = bullish. Near upper band = possible breakout or exhaustion. Width indicates volatility regime.

Period: 20 | Std Dev: 2.0
ATR (Risk Only)

Average True Range measures volatility. Used to set stop-losses and profit targets dynamically — wider ATR = wider stops but also larger targets. Not scored directly.

Period: 14 | Stop: 2.0× ATR

Relative Volume (RVOL)

RVOL compares current volume to the average volume at the same time of day over the past 20 sessions. This matters because volume naturally spikes at open (9:30–10:00 AM) and close (3:30–4:00 PM), and dips at midday. A simple daily average would show false positives in the morning and false negatives at noon.

RVOL(now) = Volume(now) / Avg Volume(same time of day, past 20 days)

Thresholds:
  < 1.0x → Below average (SKIP)
  1.33x → Minimum for entry consideration (v3.1 — lowered from 1.5x for wider coverage)
  2.0x+ → Strong institutional interest
  3.0x+ → Catalyst event likely

News Sentiment Scoring

Headlines are scored using VADER (Valence Aware Dictionary for Sentiment Reasoning), enhanced with a finance-specific lexicon. Words like "bullish", "beat", "upgrade" get strong positive scores; "crash", "downgrade", "miss" get negative scores.

Recency Decay

Recent news matters more. A headline from 10 minutes ago gets full weight; one from 2 hours ago gets half weight.

Weighted Sentiment = Raw Score × Recency Weight

< 15 min old → 1.0× weight
15–30 min → 0.9×
30–60 min → 0.7×
1–2 hours → 0.5×
> 2 hours → 0.3×

First-Order vs. Second-Order Impact

First-order: Direct effect on the company in the headline. Apple beats earnings → Apple stock moves. This is scored directly from the headline.

Second-order: Spillover to related companies. Apple beats earnings → semiconductor suppliers (AVGO, QCOM) benefit from implied demand strength. The scanner tracks sector peer mappings and can flag when a peer has strong recent sentiment.

Pivot Point Resistance Levels

In addition to ATR-based targets, each signal includes the nearest Standard Floor Pivot resistance level above the entry price. This gives a Barchart-style overhead resistance estimate based on the prior day's price action.

Pivot (P) = (High + Low + Close) / 3

R1 = (2 × P) − Low
R2 = P + (High − Low)
R3 = High + 2 × (P − Low)

S1 = (2 × P) − High
S2 = P − (High − Low)
S3 = Low − 2 × (High − P)

The scanner finds the nearest R1, R2, or R3 above the current entry price and displays it as the Resistance Target (purple line on the chart). This helps you gauge how much room a trade has before hitting a natural ceiling.

Risk Management Rules

RuleSettingRationale
Stop-Loss Entry − (ATR × 2.0) ATR-based stops adapt to volatility. 2× ATR gives room for normal noise while cutting losses on real reversals.
Profit Target Entry + (stop × 2.5) 2.5:1 reward-to-risk. You only need to win 30% of trades to be profitable at this ratio.
Position Sizing 1% account risk On a $100K account, max $1,000 loss per trade. Number of shares = $1,000 / stop distance.
Hard Close 3:55 PM ET No overnight holds. Overnight gaps, news, and after-hours moves create uncontrollable risk.
RVOL Gate ≥ 1.33× Low-volume moves are unreliable. RVOL filter ensures institutional participation. Lowered from 1.5× in v3.1 to capture more setups while still filtering noise.
Min Score ≥ 60/100 Only trade high-conviction setups where technicals, sentiment, and volume align.

Known Limitations

Data source failover: FMP is the primary data source for real-time quotes and 5-minute candles. If the FMP API is unreachable or returns errors, the scanner automatically falls back to yfinance (15-minute delayed data). During market hours, demo data is never shown — if both sources fail, the scan returns empty rather than stale prices.

FMP Starter plan limits: The Starter plan does not support batch-quote endpoints, so quotes are fetched one ticker at a time with rate limiting (4 calls/sec). A full S&P 100 scan takes ~25 seconds for quotes alone.

Pivot accuracy: Pivot resistance levels require at least 2 days of intraday data to calculate (prior day's H/L/C). On the first day of scanning a new ticker or after extended market closures, pivots may be unavailable.

History persistence: Daily finds are stored as JSON files on disk. On Railway (ephemeral filesystem), history is lost on redeploy. For persistent storage, a Railway volume mount is needed.

News coverage: Finnhub free tier covers major headlines but may miss niche or breaking news. Sentiment scoring is keyword-based (VADER), not deep-learning. It catches obvious sentiment but may miss sarcasm or complex narratives.

Not financial advice: This scanner is an educational tool. Past technical patterns don't guarantee future results. Always do your own research and consider consulting a financial advisor.
