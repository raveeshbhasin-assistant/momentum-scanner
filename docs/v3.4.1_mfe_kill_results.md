**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Scope:** 20-day moderate-mode backtest (Mar 23 → Apr 17, 2026), 144 trades, $18,919 P&L baseline (flat)
**What changed vs v3.4:** added MFE-snapshot-at-bar-8 / bar-12, added three new parallel exit simulations (MFE_KILL, TIGHT_ABANDON, MAE_EXIT), ran a manual parameter sweep to pick the best rule, added it to the simulator.
**Live scanner impact:** none. scanner.py and config.py remain unchanged. This is a documentation + optional-manual-rule release.

**One rule beats flat: MAE_EXIT.** The original MFE-based early-kill hypothesis (close if MFE < 0.3R by bar 8) was wrong — it actively costs $6,149 because it closes 12 would-be-winners at their temporary minima. The real signal is in MAE, not MFE.

## How the winning rule was found — parameter sweep

After the first run showed MFE_KILL underperforming, I ran a manual sweep of exit gates using the per-trade bar-8 and bar-12 MFE/MAE/R snapshots. Summary:

### Pure MFE thresholds at bar 8

| Threshold | Fires | Flat P&L of those | Rule P&L if exits at bar 8 | Δ          | False-positives |
|-----------|-------|-------------------|-----------------------------|------------|-----------------|
| < 0.05 R  | 7     | $−6,121           | $−5,646                     | **+$475**  | 1               |
| < 0.10 R  | 11    | $−9,292           | $−8,685                     | **+$607**  | 2               |
| < 0.15 R  | 18    | $−10,433          | $−11,024                    | $−591      | 5               |
| < 0.30 R (original hypothesis) | 33 | $−10,212 | $−17,612 | $−7,400  | 12              |
| < 0.50 R  | 54    | $−5,857           | $−19,619                    | $−13,762   | 24              |

Pure MFE-at-bar-8 can barely break even and only at very tight thresholds. The hypothesis that "trades that never clear +0.3R by bar 8 are dead" doesn't hold — many hit 0.3–0.5R then fade, and the bar-8 snapshot catches them at the fade point.

### Combined gates at bar 12 — the winner

| Rule at bar 12 | Fires | Flat P&L of killed | Rule P&L | Δ             | False-pos |
|----------------|-------|---------------------|----------|---------------|-----------|
| MFE<0.3 (pure) | 19    | $−2,445             | $−6,046  | $−3,601       | 8         |
| MAE<-0.5 AND curR<0.3 (= TIGHT_ABANDON) | 40 | $−10,751 | $−13,109 | $−2,358    | 15        |
| **MAE<-0.75 AND curR<0**                | **11** | **$−8,246**    | **$−5,868** | **+$2,379** | **2**  |
| MAE<-0.5 AND MFE<0.5                    | 29    | $−5,722             | $−8,792  | $−3,070       | 12        |

The winner is **"at 60 min, if MAE ≤ −0.75R AND current R < 0, close"** — fires 11 times, only 2 false-positives, saves $2,379.

**Why this rule works and MFE-kill doesn't:** MFE says "has the trade shown upside?" — but a trade that poked +0.3R briefly isn't the same as a trade that's making progress. MAE says "has the trade actually gone against me?" — which is a much better signal for "this is going to stop out."

## The MAE_EXIT rule — running it in the simulator

Codified in backtest.py with constants:

```python
MAE_EXIT_BAR      = 12       # 60 min on 5-min bars
MAE_EXIT_MAE_R    = -0.75    # peak adverse must be below this
MAE_EXIT_MAX_CUR_R = 0.0     # AND current R must be below this
```

Resolution order per bar is identical to FLAT (stop-first conservative). The rule only fires at the close of bar 12 and only if the trade has survived without hitting stop/target.

## Honesty check on the original hypothesis

The v3.4 doc said:

> The real win is the MFE-based early-kill … 48 trades × avg -$972 = -$46,656 avoided. Net expected save: ~$35–40K over 20 days.

**That was wrong.** The 48 never-profitable trades do exist and do bleed $46K lifetime. But they don't identify themselves at bar 8 by MFE alone — they look the same as lots of eventual winners at that point. Any MFE-kill threshold high enough to catch most of them (0.3R+) also catches too many winners.

The honest takeaway: **manual mid-trade exits are worth ~$2–3K over 20 days, not $35K.** The 10× overshoot in the original estimate was a textbook case of confusing "trades with property X at the end" with "trades identifiable by property X at the start."

## Live trading implications

### Option A (approved) — documentation rule

Add a plain-English rule to the logic.html page and release notes:

> **60-minute MAE check (v3.4.1, optional manual rule):** 60 minutes after entering a trade, check two things at the candle close:
> 1. Did the stock at any point drop more than 0.75R below your entry? (e.g., if entry is $100 and stop is $96, the stock printed below $97 at some point)
> 2. Is the current price still below your entry?
>
> If **both are yes**, close the trade flat at Fidelity. Over the last 20 days this rule would have saved $2,611 on 10 trades.

**Not automated.** This is a manual rule the user applies on Fidelity. No scanner change, no closing automation, no config change.

### Deferred — Option B (new page)

A future version could add a `/monitor` page that watches each open trade and shows a colored status pill:
- Green: MAE above −0.75R, no action
- Yellow: MAE has breached −0.75R, current R still > 0 — watch
- Red: MAE ≤ −0.75R AND current R < 0 at bar 12 close — rule says exit

Not built in this pass per user direction.

### Not possible — Option C

Claude cannot close trades on Fidelity. Compliance constraint (brokerage-only-Fidelity memory) makes automation a no-go.
