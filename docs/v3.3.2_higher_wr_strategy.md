# Path to higher WR — strategy doc (v3.3.2 → v3.4)

**Date:** 2026-04-18
**Based on:** 20-day moderate-mode backtest (Mar 23 → Apr 17), 126 trades, $15,086 P&L, 26.2% decided-WR / 46% PnL-positive

## The honest picture the data is painting

The "26.2% WR" headline masks a much more interesting truth. Here's the full exit breakdown:

| Exit     | n   | P&L        | Avg R  | Avg bars held |
|----------|-----|------------|--------|---------------|
| TARGET   | 21  | +$49,755   | +2.35  | 31.5 (~2h45)  |
| EOD win  | 37  | +$27,541   | +0.76  | 52.7 (~4h25)  |
| EOD loss | 9   |  -$3,073   | -0.33  | 59.7          |
| STOP     | 59  | -$59,137   | -1.03  | 16.5 (~1h25)  |

- **True profitable rate = 58/126 = 46%.** The "26.2%" only counts TARGET hits.
- **Only 7.1% of trades actually hit the 2.5R target. 17.5% hit ≥2R at final. 21.4% hit ≥1.5R.**
- **18 stop-outs were held >100 min and lost $17,906** — these are "winners that gave it back," the single largest P&L leak.
- **Losers die fast (median 67 min), winners cook slow (median 227 min).** There's a clean time-signature.
- **Apr 17 alone lost ~$18K** across 30 trades. Equity peaked at $33.8K on Apr 16 and bled back to $15K.

## Counterintuitive findings worth building against

### 1. Higher composite score → worse WR
| Score  | n  | WR    | Avg R  | Why |
|--------|----|-------|--------|-----|
| 60–64  | 82 | 48.8% | +0.26  | Normal setups |
| 65–69  | 40 | 42.5% | -0.16  | Slightly worse |
| 70–74  |  4 | 25.0% | -0.30  | Already-moved |
| 75+    |  0 |   —   |   —    | Never fires (theoretical cap) |

**Avg RVOL climbs with score: 60–64 = 2.22x, 70+ = 3.31x.** High score = everything aligned *because the move already happened*. Classic late-picker trap.

### 2. RVOL sweet spot is NOT "more is better"
| RVOL     | n  | WR    |
|----------|----|-------|
| 1.33–2x  | 56 | 48.2% |
| 2–3x     | 44 | 52.3% ← best |
| 3–5x     | 19 | 21.1% ← trap |
| 5x+      |  7 | 57.1% |

Parabolic 3–5x RVOL looks exciting but is where we catch reversals. 5x+ is usually a major catalyst → different animal.

### 3. Sector concentration is extreme
Best: AI/Cloud 45%, Comm Services 100% (n=2), Real Estate 67% (n=4), Software 40%
Worst: Semis 9%, Industrials 10%, Consumer Staples 0% (n=6), Crypto/Blockchain 0% (n=4)

Semis and Crypto are systematic losers over 20 days.

### 4. Repeat offenders
Tickers with multiple full-loss runs this window: **CRML (0/3), APLD (0/2), OKLO (0/2), CB (0/2), BDX (0/2).** Combined: -$10,969.

## Recommendations — ranked by expected impact × effort

The user's note — *"not just screen more"* — is the right instinct. The screening layer is already doing a lot. The real gaps are in **exit logic**, **entry timing**, and **information we're not capturing**.

---

### 🔴 TIER 1 — Exit logic overhaul (highest impact)

**1.1 Track MFE / MAE per trade in the backtest** — prerequisite for everything else.
Currently we record only the final exit R. We need per-bar tracking of the peak favorable and peak adverse price inside each trade. This is 30 lines in backtest.py and unlocks every other exit-logic decision.

**1.2 Tiered partial exits + breakeven stop**
- 33% off at +1R → stop moves to breakeven
- 33% off at +1.75R → trail stop at prior 15-min swing low
- 33% runs to target (2.5R) or EOD close

Even under conservative assumptions (only trades that currently hit ≥1R in the data), this turns ~32 of today's STOP-outs into scratches or small wins and locks in partial profits on trades that currently don't qualify as "wins." Expected decided-WR lift from 26% → ~40%. Expected PnL lift from flat-stops that currently become -1R.

**1.3 Time-based abandonment**
If after 12 bars (60 min) the trade is inside ±0.5R and not trending, exit flat. The 18 long-held stops that bled $17,906 all fit this profile — they sat in chop, then rolled over.

---

### 🟡 TIER 2 — Entry timing (addresses the late-picker problem)

**2.1 Move-extension penalty in the composite**
Encodes the score-WR inversion finding directly:
- If `close > VWAP + 2 × ATR` → score -15
- If `close > pre-market high + 1 × ATR` → score -10
- If `close > 5-day high + 1 × ATR` → score -10

High-tech-score + high-RVOL setups will fail this gate because they're, by definition, the extended ones.

**2.2 Pullback-confirmation entry**
Scan picks the candidate. Don't enter at scan close. Wait up to 2 bars for a tap of VWAP or EMA9. Enter on the rejection wick. If no pullback in 10 minutes, skip.
- Expected to filter ~30% of current trades (the extended ones).
- Trades that do enter get in 0.3–0.7 ATR tighter → stops are smaller in absolute terms → position sizing can scale → R improves mechanically.

---

### 🟢 TIER 3 — Adaptive calibration (not "more filters" — per-ticker learning)

**3.1 Rolling per-ticker WR gate**
Track each ticker's trailing 10-trade WR. Tickers under 30% over 10+ trades require composite ≥ 70 to enter. Tickers with 0 wins in last 3 decided trades → 5-day cooldown.

This is not adding a screen — it's **letting the system calibrate to its own blind spots**. CRML, APLD, OKLO would be on cooldown today. If they rehab, they come back automatically.

**3.2 Sector-of-day classifier (live breadth)**
Current sector assignment is static. Build: each morning, after 30 min of trading, compute each sector's advance/decline ratio and median %change. Top-3 sectors = priority. Bottom-3 = require composite ≥ 70.

This would have stopped most Apr 17 damage — the day was a broad-market slide and we kept firing new entries. Sector-breadth check would've flagged "no green sectors, halt new entries."

---

### 🔵 TIER 4 — New capabilities (match the project brief)

**4.1 Resistance-aware targets** (explicitly what the project brief asks for)
Replace flat 2.5R target with `min(2.5R, nearest_resistance - 0.5 ATR)`. Resistance sources, ordered:
- Prior day high / pre-market high
- Round numbers ($100, $150, etc.)
- 5-day and 20-day highs
- VWAP + 2 ATR band top

More targets get hit because the target is realistic. Avg R per winner drops slightly, but TARGET-hit rate likely doubles.

**4.2 News-driven priority lane (first-order / second-order)**
Matches project brief: *"leverage news and its first order and second order impact."* We have Finnhub + VADER but only use it as 25% of composite. Build:
- Catalyst freshness score — news in last 2h, bullish sentiment, pre-market gap >2% = +15 composite boost AND priority scan slot.
- Second-order: if a ticker's *peer* has a catalyst (e.g., NVDA earnings beat → AMD, AVGO get a boost for 2 hours), apply a decay-weighted peer-catalyst bonus.

**4.3 "Why I missed this" EOD audit**
Nightly job: fetch top 20 gainers of the day, cross-reference scanner logs. Report: *"AAOI +8.4% — rejected at 10:00 because RVOL was 1.28 < 1.33 threshold."* Over 2–3 weeks this reveals the actual blind spots behind the "my list had more winners" complaint, rather than guessing.

**4.4 Bad-day detector ("Apr 17 mode")**
When breadth collapses (e.g., <30% S&P 500 members green, VIX +1.5 pts intraday, >60% of scanned tickers fail leader filter), flip scanner to **defense mode**: halt new entries, tighten all open trades to breakeven-or-better. Built-in circuit breaker.

## Suggested build order

1. **Tier 1.1 (MFE/MAE tracking)** — 30 min. Invisible change that unlocks everything else.
2. **Tier 1.2 (tiered exits)** — backtest-only first, validate the lift.
3. **Tier 2.1 (move-extension penalty)** — cheap, testable, should help noticeably.
4. **Tier 3.1 (per-ticker rolling WR)** — low effort, surgical.
5. **Tier 4.4 (bad-day detector)** — prevents Apr-17 repeats.
6. **Tier 4.1 (resistance-aware targets)** — the project-brief one.
7. **Tier 2.2 (pullback entry)** — biggest behavior change; save for after infrastructure.
8. **Tier 3.2, 4.2, 4.3** — infra-heavy, defer to v3.5.

## What I'd do first

**If I could only build one thing: Tier 1.1 + 1.2 together.**
The MFE/MAE data lets us prove the tiered-exit math honestly before any other change. Every other improvement (resistance targets, move penalty, pullback entry) rides on top of better exit logic. If we fix the exit leak, +$15K becomes +$30K at the same screening quality.
