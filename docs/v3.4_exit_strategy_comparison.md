## Why each exit strategy has the P&L it does

**TIERED left $21,018 on the table from strong winners.**
Of 24 trades that hit ≥2R MFE, flat earned $54,836; tiered earned $33,818. Example: GILD hit target +2.26R for $1,787 flat → tiered earned $1,293 (banked 33% at 1R + 33% at 1.75R, only 33% rode to target). The tiered WR gain comes almost entirely from banking 1R partials on trades that would have stopped out — but it sacrifices a chunk of every winner to do so.

**COMBO (abandon + tiered) killed slow-brewing winners.**
Two clear cases:
- **BKNG 2026-04-06**: flat hit target at $+1,847; combo abandoned at chop at bar 12 for $-243. MFE eventually +2.50R at bar 61. The trade was slow to develop.
- **BRK-B 2026-04-09**: flat hit target at $+2,395; combo abandoned at chop for $+153. MFE +2.36R at bar 27.

The 1.3 rule as currently implemented (±0.5R window at bar 12) is **too aggressive** — it can't distinguish "chop that will resolve up" from "chop that will stop out."

**ABANDON alone costs $4,760.**
The abandon rule cut some losers (good) but also cut some eventual winners (bad). Net $-4,760. Saves $7K on the never-profitable cohort specifically, but offsets that elsewhere.

## What we learned about the trade-off

| Strategy | Good for              | Bad for                |
|----------|-----------------------|------------------------|
| FLAT     | Maximum P&L           | Large drawdowns (-$20K max) |
| TIERED   | WR consistency, lower DD | Leaves $21K on strong winners |
| ABANDON  | Dramatic drawdown cut | Kills slow winners (-$5K net) |
| COMBO    | Best drawdown ($10.5K = half of flat) | Worst P&L hit    |

The abandon rule in its current form doesn't earn its P&L cost because it closes some trades that would have recovered. But its **drawdown impact is real** — max DD went from $19,775 to $10,515.

## Recommendation: v3.4.1 tune the abandon rule, don't default to tiered

The headline asks — tiered exits, 1.3 rule, redefine win — are all done and measured. The data now tells us:

1. **Tiered as a default would halve P&L.** Not worth it. Keep as an optional mode for users who want the smoother equity curve.

2. **The 1.3 rule needs to be tighter.** Current trigger: `bar 12 AND |r| < 0.5R`. Better: `bar 12 AND MAE < -0.5R AND current_r < +0.3R`. This says "only abandon if the trade has actually gone against us, not just sat flat." That should preserve slow winners while still cutting the true chop-to-stop trades.

3. **The real win is the MFE-based early-kill.** The 48 never-profitable trades have avg MFE +0.18R by the time they die. If we close any trade whose MFE has not exceeded +0.3R by bar 8 (40 min), we'd catch most of the bleed. Roughly:
   - 48 trades × avg -$972 = -$46,656 avoided
   - Minus ~5 false-positives that would have recovered
   - Net expected save: ~$35–40K over 20 days

That's an order of magnitude more impact than any tiered-exit variation.
